1. The Power of POD: Why 2025 is the Year?

Print on Demand (POD) is the ultimate risk-free business. In 2025, you don't need a factory or a warehouse. You just need a **Design**. When a customer buys a T-shirt on your site, the printing company prints it and ships it. You never touch the product, but you keep the profit. This is the **Scalable Creativity** model.

2. Choosing a Winning Niche (Beyond "Funny Quotes")

Funny quotes are saturated. You need a **Hyper-Niche**. Examples:

  • The Specialized Hobbyist: "Bonsai Tree Enthusiasts" or "Mechanical Keyboard Nerds".
  • The Local Pride: "Bengaluru Techie Vibes" or "Punjabi Power Quotes".
  • The Profession-Based: "Code Debugging Humor for Python Devs".

3. Design for Non-Designers (The AI Secret)

You don't need Photoshop. In 2025, you use **Midjourney** and **Canva**.

The 30-Second Design Process:

  1. Use Midjourney to generate a unique artistic graphic.
  2. Use Vectorizer.ai to make it high-resolution.
  3. Use Canva to add typography and finalize the layout.

6. Hacking Instagram & TikTok for Viral Sales

Don't post product photos. Post **Vibes**. Use AI to create "Mockups" of people wearing your shirts in cool locations. Create 15-second Reels using trending audio. The goal is to make people say "I need this" within 3 seconds.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

What is the profit margin?

Typically 30-50%. If a shirt costs ₹400 to print and ship, you sell it for ₹799 - ₹899.

How do I handle returns?

Most POD companies only handle returns for defective items. For size changes, you must clearly state your policy. Many pros offer a "No Return" policy unless damaged to keep margins high.
