1. The Secret World of Wholesaling: Why No One Talks About It

Most people think you need crores to make money in real estate. They are wrong. Wholesaling is the art of **Contract Arbitrage**. You are not buying the property; you are buying the **Right to Buy** the property, and then selling that right to an investor. In India, where real estate is the #1 asset class, being the middleman who finds "deals" is a gold mine.

2. The 70% Rule: The Math of a Good Deal

To attract an investor, the deal must have enough margin. Use the **70% Rule**:

The Formula:

Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO) = (ARV x 0.70) - Repairs - Your Fee

  • ARV (After Repair Value): What the house is worth in perfect condition.
  • 0.70: The investor needs a 30% safety margin.
  • Repairs: Estimated cost to fix the place.
  • Your Fee: What you want to earn (e.g., ₹5 Lakhs).

3. Finding Motivated Sellers (The "Driving for Dollars" Method)

You don't want a "Happy Seller". You want a **Motivated Seller**. Look for:

  • Distressed Properties: Houses with peeling paint, tall grass, or junk in the yard.
  • Inherited Properties: Owners who live in another city and just want the cash.
  • Divorce or Financial Crisis: People who need to sell within 30 days.

Pro Tip: Use Google Maps to "virtually" drive around older neighborhoods in cities like Pune, Bangalore, or Noida to find neglected properties.

5. The Assignment Clause: Making it Legal in India

This is the most critical part. Your "Agreement to Sell" MUST include the words:

"This agreement is assignable to any third-party buyer at the sole discretion of the Purchaser without any additional consent required from the Seller."

This allows you to legally sell your contract to an investor for an **Assignment Fee**.

6. Building Your Cash Buyer List

A wholesaler is only as good as their buyers. You need a list of 50-100 real estate investors who have cash ready. How to find them?

  • Go to property auctions.
  • Call the "For Rent" numbers on property sites—these are usually landlords/investors.
  • Network with local real estate lawyers and accountants.

9. Frequently Asked Questions

Do I need a RERA license?

For large-scale brokerage, yes. For wholesaling individual contracts, it's often a gray area, but it's highly recommended to register as a consultant to be 100% compliant as you scale.

What if I can't find a buyer?

Always include an "Inspection Period" or "Contingency" in your contract. If you can't find a buyer in 15 days, you have the right to cancel the contract without losing your token amount.
