The "Indian Paradox": High Volume, High Returns

Dropshipping in India is not like dropshipping in the US. In the US, you worry about shipping times. In India, you worry about RTO (Return to Origin). Because India is a Cash-on-Delivery (COD) market, up to 30% of your customers might simply refuse to pick up the order at their doorstep. If you don't solve this, you will lose money.

Phase 1: Sourcing "Winning" Products

In 2025, generic fidget spinners won't work. You need products that solve a "Pain Point" or have a "Wow Factor".

Winning Product Examples:

  • Home Security: Wireless bulb cameras for ₹999 (Sourced for ₹400).
  • Kitchen Tech: Portable rechargeable blenders for ₹1,299 (Sourced for ₹500).
  • Health/Posture: Back support belts for ₹799 (Sourced for ₹250).

Phase 2: The COD Confirmation System (Anti-RTO)

This is the difference between a failing store and a ₹5 Lakh/month store. You MUST confirm every order.

The 3-Step Confirmation Protocol:

  1. Automatic WhatsApp: Send an instant message asking for "1" to confirm or "2" to cancel.
  2. IVR Call: Use a service like CloudTalk to send an automated call.
  3. Manual Double-Check: For high-value orders, have a virtual assistant call and verify the address landmarks.

Goal: Reduce RTO from 35% to less than 15%.

Phase 3: Meta Ads Scaling Blueprint

Don't "target" interests. Let the Pixel do the work.

  • CBO (Campaign Budget Optimization): Start with ₹1,000/day.
  • Creative: Use UGC (User Generated Content). Real people filming the product on their phone works 5x better than studio ads in India.
  • Hook: Show the problem in the first 3 seconds.

Professional FAQ

Which supplier should I use?

GlowRoad (owned by Amazon) and Roposo Cloud are the best for beginners. They handle the shipping and COD collection for you.

Is GST mandatory to start?

Technically, no (if below ₹20L/year turnover), but most payment gateways and suppliers in India will ask for it. It's better to get a GST registration (Sole Proprietorship) for ₹1,500 - ₹2,000.
