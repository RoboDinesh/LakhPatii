1. The Death of Dine-In: Why Cloud Kitchens?

The traditional restaurant model in India is broken. Between high rent, electrical bills, and staff turnover, most restaurants fail in 18 months. **Cloud Kitchens** (Delivery-only) solve this by removing the expensive "Experience" and focusing only on the **Product**. You can run a kitchen that does ₹5 Lakhs in monthly sales from a ₹15,000/month garage space.

2. Choosing Your Food Niche

Don't be a "Multi-Cuisine" restaurant. Be the best at one thing. Here are the 2025 "Winner" niches:

  • The Healthy Bowl: High margins, targeted at corporate employees.
  • Gourmet Burgers: Low ingredient cost, high perceived value.
  • Authentic Regional (e.g., Authentic Kerala or Malvani): No competition in North/West India.
  • The Dessert Jar: Viral potential on Instagram.

3. The 3 Essential Licenses (Non-Negotiable)

FSSAI Registration

The core food safety license. Costs ~₹100 for a basic one. Mandatory for Swiggy/Zomato.

GST Registration

Necessary once you scale or if you want to claim credit on your raw material purchases.

Trade License

Issued by your local municipal corporation. Varies by state.

5. Menu Engineering: Pricing for Profit

Your food cost should be exactly 25-30% of your selling price. If a Biryani sells for ₹300, it must cost you less than ₹90 to make (including ingredients, gas, and oil).

The "Decoy" Strategy:

Have a "Premium" item at ₹500 that you don't expect to sell much. It makes your "Standard" ₹300 item look like a great deal. This is how pros increase their Average Order Value (AOV).

8. The "Packaging" Secret: Your Only Marketing

In a cloud kitchen, the customer never sees you. The **Packaging** is your only physical touchpoint. Don't use generic plastic. Use branded boxes, include a "Thank You" note, and maybe a small piece of candy. This simple trick increases your 5-star review rate by 40%.

10. Frequently Asked Questions

How much commission does Zomato take?

Typically 20-25% per order. You must account for this in your pricing. If you sell at ₹200, Zomato takes ₹50, leaving you with ₹150.

Can I start from my apartment kitchen?

Technically yes, but once you hit 10+ orders an hour, your home setup will fail. It's better to rent a small dedicated commercial space for ₹5,000-₹10,000 to keep your home and business separate.
